Enclave

For a 10-week part-time UX Design course at General Assembly, I worked on an app of my own creation. The result is Enclave, a way for gender non-conforming people to find and share their favorite spaces.

The Challenge

Gender non-conforming (GNC*) people need a way to find places to go without compromising their physical or psychological safety.

*For those not familiar with the term, GNC refers to anyone whose appearance, actions and/or gender identity don't match with cultural norms based on their biological sex.


The Approach

A mobile app which allows gender non-conforming (GNC*) people to find and share experiences of any space, public or private.


Goals

  • Identify a problem which is important and lacking a solution today.
  • Identify the target audience for the stated problem, and understand their wants, needs, priorities and pain points.
  • Design the MVP for a product to address the needs of the target audience, utilizing modern design and UX principles.

Duration

10 weeks


Tools

Pen, Paper, Index Cards, Post-It Notes, Sketch, InVision, Figma, OptimalWorkshop


My Role

User Researcher, UX Designer, UI Designer

Design Brief

There are many apps available today which allow anyone the ability to rate and review anyplace. Seriously - anyplace. However, these apps are focused on sharing information which would apply to anyone generically; they don't support the particular interests and needs of more niche audiences, in this case GNC people. My project sought to address this challenge by proposing a way for GNC people to share the information most relevant to their needs.

Phase 1: User Research

Primary Research: Interviews

I started by interviewing a number of GNC friends and colleagues using the following research guide


Problem Statement

The problem I suspect is GNC people feel uncomfortable in public, gendered spaces.


Purpose

Understand in what spaces trans/non-binary/gender non-conforming people feel comfortable vs uncomfortable.


Research Outcome(s)

  • What public spaces to participants consider gendered?
  • What contributes to participants feeling comfortable vs uncomfortable?
  • How do participants navigate unfamiliar locations?

Method(s)

In-person and remote interviews


Measures

Location, Ethnicity, Gender Identity


Participants

Adults identifying as gender non-conforming, living in a major US city.


Going into the interviews, I recognized that my definition of a "gendered" space may not align with that of my interviewees (you are not the customer!). My definition was an assumption I needed to check, and thus I focused on learning which spaces made my interviewees comfortable vs un-comfortable without any limiting factors on the type. From that I hoped to get insight into what a more encompassing definition of gendered spaces may be.


Research Insight: When you're a gender minority, the world is a gendered place.

My interviews revealed that I was indeed off base with my internal definition of gendered spaces. Great! Interviews, like usability tests, are most interesting and valuable when they reveal that you would have gone in the wrong direction without them.


Secondary Research: Competitive Analysis

I also conducted a competitive analysis of several other services and apps available which allow for sharing information and/or social connections. I utilized a simple 2x2 to organize these competitors by demographic ("For Everyone" vs "GNC specific") and type of sharing ("Social Network" vs "Sharing/Reviews/Ratings"). This analysis was valuable in revealing that there really isn't a product available today geared at GNC people for sharing specific ratings or reviews.


User Personas

Based on the interviews and research, I came up with a primary user persona (Alex), which I used to guide the development of the app going forward. I also created an updated problem statement based on the persona.

Problem Statement

Alex needs a way to find places other GNC people frequent because those places are most likely to provide a feeling of psychological and physical safety.
